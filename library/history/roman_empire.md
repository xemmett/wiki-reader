# Roman Empire

The Roman Empire was the post-Republican period of ancient Rome. As a polity, it
included large territorial holdings around the Mediterranean Sea in Europe, North
Africa, and Western Asia, ruled by emperors.

## Rise

From the accession of Augustus in 27 BC, Rome grew from a city-state into an
empire spanning three continents. Roman roads, aqueducts, and law bound the
territories together and shaped European civilization for centuries.

## Decline

The Western Roman Empire collapsed in 476 AD, while the Eastern half, later known
as the Byzantine Empire, endured until 1453. Historians still debate the causes:
economic strain, military overreach, and political instability all played parts.
