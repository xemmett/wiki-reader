# Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the American spaceflight that first landed humans on the Moon.
Commander Neil Armstrong and lunar module pilot Buzz Aldrin landed the Apollo
Lunar Module Eagle on 20 July 1969.

## The Landing

Armstrong became the first person to step onto the lunar surface six hours and
39 minutes later, on 21 July. Aldrin joined him about 20 minutes later. They
spent about two and a quarter hours together outside the spacecraft and
collected 47.5 pounds of lunar material to bring back to Earth.

## Significance

The mission fulfilled a national goal set by President John F. Kennedy in 1961:
landing a man on the Moon and returning him safely to Earth before the decade
was out. It remains one of the defining achievements of the twentieth century.
